But, what is an ultrasound?

Definition of ultrasounds

First thing first: what is precisely an ultrasound?

What is an ultrasound?

An ultrasound is a mechanical wave (ie. cyclic variation of air pressure over time and space).

Amplitude, frequency, period

The ultrasounds are characterised by two values: amplitude and frequency:

  • the amplitude is the highest pressure obtained at the peak of the wave
  • the frequency is the number of entire cycles per time unit. Frequency is mostly measured in Hertz (Hz), indicating the number of cycles per second.

The frequency is related to the period of the wave, which is the duration of a cycle. The frequency and period are related:

\[ Frequency = \frac{1}{Period} \]

For example, a wave with a frequency of 2 Hz (meaning, two cycles per second) has a period of 0.5 seconds (\(\frac{1}{2}\)). A wave with a frequency of 40 Hz has a period of 0.025 seconds (\(\frac{1}{40}\)) or 25 milliseconds.

Energy of the ultrasounds

The energy of an ultrasound depends on both the frequency and the amplitude:

  • the higher the frequency, the higher the energy
  • the higher the amplitude, the higher the energy
Why it matters?

Choosing wisely the frequency and amplitude is essential to obtain the best image quality while limiting the potential adverse effects (see after).
